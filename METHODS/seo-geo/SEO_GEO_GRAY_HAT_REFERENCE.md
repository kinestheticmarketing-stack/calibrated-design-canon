# SEO_GEO_GRAY_HAT_REFERENCE.md

*Honest documentation of gray-hat SEO tactics for informed operator decisions.*

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
POSTURE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This document treats the operator as an adult. Gray-hat tactics exist, some work, some are being actively hunted, some are dead but still being sold in courses. The playbook documents what they are, how they work, what the current risk profile is, and why the canon position is conservative.

The canon does not endorse most of these tactics. The canon also does not pretend they don't exist or that operators choosing them are doing something incomprehensible. The canon explains the tradeoffs honestly and lets operators choose with full information.

The Calibrated Stack's substance discipline (no fabrication, no fake schema, no fake reviews) is non-negotiable regardless of which path the operator chooses. See `SEO_GEO_PLAYBOOK.md` for forbidden practices.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
TACTIC CATEGORIES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Each tactic is categorized as:

- **ENDORSED** — White-hat or gray-adjacent, the playbook supports careful execution.
- **ACKNOWLEDGED** — Works, carries real but manageable risk, operator-level decision.
- **DOCUMENTED-NOT-RECOMMENDED** — Works, the playbook explicitly doesn't endorse due to risk-reward profile.
- **FORBIDDEN** — Violates canon substance rules regardless of effectiveness.

TACTIC FORMAT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Each tactic entry includes:

- **Name** — short identifier.
- **Category** — Endorsed / Acknowledged / Documented-Not-Recommended / Forbidden.
- **Durability** — Durable / Frontier / Dead (with date of last review).
- **What it is** — concise description of the tactic.
- **How it works** — implementation detail at level required to understand the choice.
- **Conservative alternative** — the white-hat path that achieves a similar goal.
- **What you're risking** — specific consequences if the tactic is detected.
- **Currently being hunted?** — Google enforcement status.
- **Sources** — courses/practitioners documenting the tactic.
- **Playbook position** — conservative recommendation, with explicit risk framing for operators who choose the aggressive path.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ENFORCEMENT WATCHLIST
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Tactics currently subject to active Google enforcement (as of build date):

- **Third-party parasite SEO** — Google's "site reputation abuse" policy enforcement, launched May 2024. Big publisher domains hosting third-party content actively de-indexed.
- **Mass AI content** — Helpful Content Updates (2022-2024) effectively killed scaled AI publishing. Sites doing this have lost 60-90% of traffic.
- **Expired domain redirects (mass)** — Degraded significantly 2023-2024; less effective than rebuild + link.
- **GBP listing keyword stuffing** — Easily reported, fast manual penalty.
- **Exact-match anchor text overuse** — Penguin-era enforcement still active.
- **Fake reviews / fake AggregateRating** — Schema-validation suppression at minimum, manual penalty risk.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
DEAD TACTICS (do not use, but be aware they're still being sold)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

- Mass AI content publishing (HCU killed it)
- Third-party parasite SEO on rented authority subdomains (Site Reputation Abuse policy)
- Expired-domain mass 301 redirects (degraded heavily)
- Exact-match domain advantage at high-competition (gone since ~2015 broadly)
- Keyword density optimization (gone since BERT, 2019)
- Footer/sidebar link networks (gone since Penguin, 2012)
