# Naming Canon

The Calibrated Design Apps franchise is named in Old Norse. Each app
is named after the function it performs.

## Current franchise

| App    | Old Norse  | Pronunciation | Meaning                  | Function          |
|--------|------------|---------------|--------------------------|-------------------|
| STUND  | stund      | STOOND        | a moment, a while        | Countdown timer   |
| SKEID  | skeið      | SKAID         | a span, a course         | Stopwatch         |
| MERKI  | merki      | MER-key       | a mark, a sign           | Unit converter    |
| HJALI  | hjali      | HYAH-lee      | chat, conversation       | Translator        |
| LJOS   | ljós       | LYOOS         | light                    | Flashlight        |

## Convention

- **Operational names** (folders, repos, App Store listings, URLs):
  ASCII only. STUND, SKEID, MERKI, HJALI, LJOS.
- **Visual brand marks** (logos, splash screens): may use Old Norse
  diacritics. SKEIÐ, LJÓS.

## Why Old Norse function-words

Coherent brand register. Each app gets dignity by being named for
what it does, not for something cute or marketing-driven. Norse
cachet adds cultural depth without pretension.

## Naming process (for future apps)

1. Identify the function in plain English (timer, counter, etc.)
2. Find the Old Norse word for that function
3. Verify App Store availability (search exact match)
4. Verify trademark / domain availability
5. Lock the name; assign ASCII operational form and diacritic visual form
